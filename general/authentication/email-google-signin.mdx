Odin AI supports traditional email/password authentication and Google Sign-In for convenient and secure access to the platform.

Odin AI provides two primary authentication methods for individual users:

- **Email/Password** - Traditional email and password authentication
- **Google Sign-In** - Sign in with your Google account

## Email/Password Authentication
Email/Password authentication allows users to create accounts and sign in using their email address and a password. This is the standard authentication method for most users.

### Signing Up

1. **Navigate to Sign Up**
   - Click **Sign Up** on the login page
   - Or visit the sign-up page directly

2. **Enter Your Information**
   - Enter your email address
   - Create a secure password
   - Confirm your password (if required)

3. **Complete Sign-Up**
   - Click **Sign Up** or **Create Account**
   - Verify your email address (if required)
   - Complete any additional setup steps

### Signing In

1. **Navigate to Login**
   - Go to the Odin AI login page
   - Enter your email address
   - Enter your password

2. **Authenticate**
   - Click **Sign In** or **Log In**
   - You'll be redirected to your dashboard upon successful authentication

### Password Requirements

Odin AI enforces password security requirements:

- **Minimum Length** - Passwords must meet minimum length requirements
- **Complexity** - May include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
- **Security** - Passwords are securely hashed and salted
- **Storage** - Passwords are never stored in plain text

### Password Reset

If you forget your password:

1. **Request Password Reset**
   - Click **Forgot Password** on the login page
   - Enter your email address
   - Click **Send Reset Link**

2. **Check Your Email**
   - Look for the password reset email
   - Click the reset link in the email

3. **Create New Password**
   - Enter your new password
   - Confirm your new password
   - Click **Reset Password**

4. **Sign In**
   - Use your new password to sign in

### Account Security

**Best Practices:**
- Use a strong, unique password
- Don't share your password with anyone
- Change your password regularly
- Use different passwords for different services
- Enable two-factor authentication if available

## Google Sign-In

### Overview

Google Sign-In allows users to authenticate using their Google account, eliminating the need to remember a separate password for Odin AI.

### How It Works

1. **Click Sign in with Google**
   - On the login page, click **Sign in with Google**
   - Or click the Google Sign-In button

2. **Select Google Account**
   - Choose your Google account from the list
   - Or enter your Google email address

3. **Grant Permissions**
   - Review the permissions requested by Odin AI
   - Click **Allow** or **Continue** to grant access

4. **Automatic Sign-In**
   - You'll be automatically signed in to Odin AI
   - Redirected to your dashboard

### Benefits

- **No Password Required** - Use your existing Google account
- **Quick Access** - Faster sign-in process
- **Secure** - Leverages Google's security infrastructure
- **Single Account** - One Google account for multiple services
- **Automatic Updates** - Profile information synced from Google

### Requirements

- **Google Account** - A valid Google account
- **Internet Connection** - Required for Google authentication
- **Browser Support** - Modern browser with JavaScript enabled
- **Permissions** - Permission to use Google Sign-In

### First-Time Setup

When signing in with Google for the first time:

1. **Account Creation**
   - Odin AI automatically creates an account using your Google email
   - Your profile information is synced from Google

2. **Profile Completion**
   - You may be asked to complete additional profile information
   - Some settings may need to be configured

3. **Access Granted**
   - You'll have full access to Odin AI features
   - Your account is linked to your Google account

### Managing Google Sign-In

**Linking Accounts:**
- If you already have an email/password account, you can link it to Google Sign-In
- This allows you to use either authentication method

**Unlinking Accounts:**
- You can unlink your Google account if needed
- You'll need to set up email/password authentication first

### Troubleshooting

**Problem**: Google Sign-In button not appearing

**Solutions:**
- Check your internet connection
- Clear browser cache and cookies
- Try a different browser
- Disable browser extensions that might block it
- Check if JavaScript is enabled

**Problem**: Permission denied or error during sign-in

**Solutions:**
- Check that you're using a valid Google account
- Verify your Google account is active
- Try signing out and signing back in
- Check browser pop-up blockers
- Contact support if issues persist

**Problem**: Account not created after Google Sign-In

**Solutions:**
- Verify your email address is correct
- Check if an account already exists with that email
- Try signing in with email/password if account exists
- Contact support for assistance

## Choosing an Authentication Method

### Email/Password

**Best For:**
- Users who prefer traditional authentication
- Organizations without SSO
- Users who want a dedicated Odin AI account
- Maximum control over account credentials

**Considerations:**
- Need to remember password
- Password management required
- Email verification may be required

### Google Sign-In

**Best For:**
- Users who already have Google accounts
- Quick and convenient access
- Users who prefer OAuth authentication
- Simplified account management

**Considerations:**
- Requires Google account
- Dependent on Google services
- Internet connection required

## Security Considerations

### Email/Password Security

- **Strong Passwords** - Always use strong, unique passwords
- **Password Storage** - Passwords are securely hashed
- **Account Lockout** - Accounts may be locked after failed attempts
- **Email Verification** - Email verification may be required

### Google Sign-In Security

- **Google Security** - Leverages Google's security infrastructure
- **Two-Factor Authentication** - Can use Google's 2FA
- **Account Protection** - Google's account protection features apply
- **Session Management** - Managed by Google's authentication system
